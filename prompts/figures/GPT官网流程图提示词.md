# GPT 官网流程图提示词

## 统一设计选择

五张流程图统一模仿 `PPT作图——3张_01.png` 的期刊信息图风格：分区清楚、层级丰富、图标克制、主流程与分支流程同时可见。画幅按最终拼版槽位分别设定：1A、1B、3A 为 9.0 × 4.5，2A 为 13.5 × 4.5，4A 为 4.5 × 4.5。以下五段提示词均可分别直接粘贴到 GPT 官网生成图片；最终以 PPT 手工临摹文字、框线和图标，不直接把生成图作为投稿原图。

统一颜色语义如下，任何一张图都不得改变：

- Human：深蓝 `#313E96`，浅填充 `#D9DCF1`。
- MAS / UroEMAS：陶土红 `#B86758`，浅填充 `#F2DFDB`。
- 评价或统计分析模块：紫色 `#7C5CFF`，浅填充 `#E9E2FF`。
- 安全审核模块：赭黄色 `#B8954B`，浅填充 `#F1E6C8`。
- 盲法、随机化、数据整理等中性过程：青绿色 `#2F8F83`，浅填充 `#DCEFEB`，或中性灰 `#6F6F6F` / `#E8E8E8`。
- 网格、分隔线、普通边框和正文：`#E3DFD8`、`#9E9A93`、`#3E3E3E`、`#C8C2B8`。
- 蓝色和陶土红只表示真实题目来源；Form A、Form B、评价工具、专家、学生和统计模型不得借用这两种来源色。

## Figure 1A — Overall study workflow

```text
Create one publication-quality scientific workflow infographic for a urology medical-education paper.

CANVAS AND REFERENCE STYLE
- Exact aspect ratio 2:1, landscape, corresponding to a 9.0 × 4.5 inch manuscript panel.
- Closely emulate the visual language of the supplied reference “PPT作图——3张_01.png”: three large vertical zones, rounded containers, clean medical line icons, clear hierarchy, restrained shadows, thin arrows, and dense but orderly information.
- White background. Flat vector appearance. No photorealism, no 3D rendering, no decorative texture, no logos, no robot faces, no brain imagery, and no unnecessary gradients.
- Put panel label “(A)” at the upper-left. Main title: “Study workflow: Human versus UroEMAS examination items”.
- All visible text must be English, correctly spelled, horizontally oriented, and large enough to trace in PowerPoint.

COLOR SEMANTICS — DO NOT DEVIATE
- Human source: dark blue #313E96 with pale blue fill #D9DCF1.
- UroEMAS / MAS source: terracotta #B86758 with pale terracotta fill #F2DFDB.
- Evaluation and statistical analysis: purple #7C5CFF with pale purple fill #E9E2FF.
- Safety review: ochre #B8954B with pale ochre fill #F1E6C8.
- Neutral processing, blinding, sampling, and data management: teal #2F8F83 with pale teal fill #DCEFEB, or neutral gray #6F6F6F with fill #E8E8E8.
- Blue and terracotta must be used only when the true source is known. When the sources are blinded, switch both paths into one neutral gray container.

LAYOUT
Use three vertical zones separated by thin dotted gray dividers. Make every arrow directional and avoid crossings.

ZONE L1 — “ITEM-BANK CONSTRUCTION”
Create two parallel source lanes.
1. Upper Human lane in blue:
   - icon: clinician-authored Word documents;
   - box text: “Human expert question documents”;
   - arrow to “Word → TXT → structured JSON”;
   - endpoint box: “Human item bank  n=775”.
2. Lower UroEMAS lane in terracotta:
   - input box: “Human bank + item-writing specifications” using a small database-and-guideline icon;
   - then three numbered connected modules:
     “1  Stems and options”,
     “2  Answer and explanation”,
     “3  Test-point reconstruction”;
   - endpoint box: “UroEMAS item bank  n=3,676”.
Keep the two lanes visually parallel and equally important. The Human lane must not appear to be generated by AI.

ZONE L2 — “SCREENING AND EXPERT EVALUATION”
Merge the two source lanes into a neutral processing pipeline while retaining small source-color tabs at the entry.
1. Box: “Machine annotation” with four compact chips beneath it:
   - “Readability”;
   - “Human–MAS similarity” with a note “MAS items only”;
   - “QGEval”;
   - “ULM”.
2. Arrow to a funnel icon and box: “First-round stratified sample  n=70/source”.
3. Arrow to a neutral blinded container: “Blinded evaluation by 3 human experts”.
4. Inside that container, show four non-overlapping tiles:
   - purple: “QGEval quality”;
   - purple: “ULM quality”;
   - ochre: “Major defects”;
   - gray: “Source-identification task”.
5. Arrow to a second funnel: “Final examination blocks  n=50/source”.

ZONE L3 — “STUDENT TESTING AND ANALYSIS”
1. At the top, show the two locked 50-item blocks side by side:
   - blue “Human block  50 items”;
   - terracotta “UroEMAS block  50 items”.
2. Feed both blocks into a neutral randomized two-sequence module:
   - “Form A: Human → UroEMAS  n=25”;
   - “Form B: UroEMAS → Human  n=25”.
   Use purple and ochre only for the Form A/Form B container outlines; keep the block colors blue and terracotta.
3. Arrow to “Student examination  n=50” with a simple examinee icon.
4. End in one purple analysis container titled “Prespecified analyses”, containing five concise rows:
   - “Expert quality and reliability”;
   - “Student score and cognitive level”;
   - “Classical test theory and KR-20”;
   - “Source detectability”;
   - “Time, cost, and fatigue”.

COMPOSITION RULES
- Preserve generous internal padding but minimize unused outer whitespace.
- Use consistent rounded-corner radii and 1–1.5 pt visual line weight.
- Use small meaningful icons only: document, database, checklist, funnel, masked/blinded label, experts, students, and chart.
- Do not invent additional sample sizes, results, P values, confidence intervals, or model names.
- Make the final graphic look like a high-impact medical journal workflow that can be accurately traced in PowerPoint.
```

## Figure 1B — Safety review workflow

```text
Create one publication-quality scientific safety-review workflow infographic for a urology medical-education paper.

CANVAS AND STYLE
- Exact 2:1 landscape ratio, corresponding to a 9.0 × 4.5 inch panel.
- Use the supplied reference “PPT作图——3张_01.png” as the visual model: layered rounded containers, compact medical line icons, strong hierarchy, thin arrows, subtle shadows only on final outcome boxes, and no visual clutter.
- White background, flat vector style, no photorealism, no logos, no robots, no decorative gradient.
- Panel label “(B)” at upper-left. Title: “Major-defect safety review and rubric validation”.
- English text only. Spell “UroEMAS”, “Human”, and “Major defect” exactly as written.

COLOR SEMANTICS
- Human source: #313E96 / #D9DCF1.
- UroEMAS source: #B86758 / #F2DFDB.
- Safety review and warning decisions: #B8954B / #F1E6C8.
- Blinding and adjudication: #2F8F83 / #DCEFEB.
- Evaluation/calibration: #7C5CFF / #E9E2FF.
- Neutral text and borders: #3E3E3E, #9E9A93, #C8C2B8.
- Do not use source colors inside blinded review boxes.

LAYOUT
Build a left-to-right main safety pipeline occupying the upper two-thirds, plus a clearly separated rubric-validation branch along the lower third. Use arrows without crossings.

MAIN PIPELINE
1. Two source boxes at far left:
   - blue: “Human first-round items  n=70”;
   - terracotta: “UroEMAS first-round items  n=70”.
2. Both arrows enter a teal neutral box:
   - title “De-identify and randomize”;
   - subtitle “Source labels hidden from reviewers”.
3. Arrow enters a large ochre safety container titled “Independent Major-defect review”.
4. Inside that container, show two reviewer lanes with simple icons:
   - “Machine review”;
   - “Human expert review”.
5. Under the reviewer lanes, show a compact seven-domain checklist in two columns, with the exact labels:
   - “1  Stem defect”;
   - “2  Option defect”;
   - “3  Format defect”;
   - “4  Answer-key / scoring defect”;
   - “5  Fairness defect”;
   - “6  Outdated-guideline defect”;
   - “7  Linked-item structural defect”.
6. Arrow to a diamond decision node: “Any educationally unacceptable defect?”
7. Split into two branches:
   - upper green-teal pass branch: “No → Pass safety gate”; beneath it write “First-round result: 0/70 in each source”.
   - lower ochre flag branch: “Yes → Assign defect code and written reason → Expert adjudication”.
8. Rejoin at a locked neutral endpoint: “Item-level safety status”.

LOWER RUBRIC-VALIDATION BRANCH
1. Start with a purple box: “Rubric validation samples”.
2. Split into two equally sized boxes:
   - “Low-score band  n=50”;
   - “Middle-score band  n=50”.
3. Add a small note beneath the pair: “Proportional sampling from Human and UroEMAS banks”.
4. Arrow to “Independent machine and human review”.
5. Arrow to a purple endpoint with two rows:
   - “Agreement and category-level error analysis”;
   - “Human adjudication pending”.

VISUAL RULES
- The seven defect categories are the visual core and must remain readable.
- Use a shield/check icon for pass, shield/exclamation for flag, and gavel or consensus icon for adjudication.
- Do not replace the seven categories with generic phrases.
- Do not imply that pending human validation results are already available.
- Minimize unused margins and keep the graphic dense, balanced, and traceable in PowerPoint.
```

```text

- Panel label “(A)” at upper-left. Title: “Blinded expert evaluation of item quality”.
- Use English only and render every label horizontally.

COLOR SEMANTICS
- Human: #313E96 with #D9DCF1 fill.
- UroEMAS: #B86758 with #F2DFDB fill.
- Evaluation/statistical modules: #7C5CFF with #E9E2FF fill.

   - blue: “Human  n=70 items”;

   - “Replace source labels with study IDs”;
```

## Figure 4A — Turing/source-identification workflow

```text
Create one publication-quality scientific workflow infographic for blinded source-identification (“Turing”) testing of Human versus UroEMAS examination items.

CANVAS AND STYLE
- Exact 1:1 square ratio for a 4.5 × 4.5 inch panel.
- Emulate the supplied “PPT作图——3张_01.png” in a compact square composition: three stacked major zones, rounded containers, reviewer and examinee icons, thin arrows, dotted separators, compact statistical endpoint cards, and no overlap.
- White background, flat vector design, no photorealism, no logos, no robot or cyborg imagery.
- Panel label “(A)” at upper-left. Title: “Blinded source-identification workflow”.
- English text only. Use “UroEMAS” consistently.

COLOR SEMANTICS
- True Human source, visible only at input and unblinding: #313E96 / #D9DCF1.
- True UroEMAS source, visible only at input and unblinding: #B86758 / #F2DFDB.
- Blinded materials and guessed labels: neutral gray #6F6F6F / #E8E8E8.
- Blinding/unblinding and linkage: teal #2F8F83 / #DCEFEB.
- Statistical analyses: purple #7C5CFF / #E9E2FF.
- Near-chance reference regions in the final accuracy tile: strict 45–55% in pale blue-purple #E1E7FF and loose 40–45% plus 55–60% in pale teal #DCEFEB.

TOP ZONE — “SOURCE-CONCEALED MATERIALS”
1. Show blue input “Human items” and terracotta input “UroEMAS items”.
2. Both enter a teal blinding module with three rows:
   - “Replace source labels with study IDs”;
   - “Randomize presentation order”;
   - “Lock the source key”.
3. Output a neutral card stack labeled “Blinded items and examination blocks”.

MIDDLE ZONE — “TWO IDENTIFICATION TASKS”
Split the middle zone into two equally prominent side-by-side cards.
1. Left card, expert item-level task:
   - heading “Expert item-level judgments”;
   - three clinician icons labeled “Expert 1”, “Expert 3”, “Expert 4”;
   - text “140 judgments per expert”;
   - each points to a binary neutral choice card: “Guess Human” or “Guess UroEMAS”.
2. Right card, student block-level task:
   - heading “Student block-level judgments”;
   - examinee group icon;
   - text “48 students with complete source judgments”;
   - binary neutral choice: “Which block was UroEMAS?”
3. Keep guessed choices gray so the graphic does not reveal the correct answer before unblinding.

BOTTOM ZONE — “UNBLINDING AND ANALYSIS”
1. Both lanes enter a teal key-unlock module: “Link each guess to the locked true source”.
2. Split into four purple endpoint cards:
   - “B  Expert accuracy with Wilson 90% CI”;
   - “C  Expert confusion matrix”;
   - “D  Mixed model for guessed UroEMAS”;
   - “E  Student accuracy with Wilson 90% CI”.
3. In the accuracy card, include a miniature horizontal probability axis with:
   - a center line at “50% chance”;
   - dark pale band labeled “Strict near-chance: 45–55%”;
   - lighter flanking bands labeled “Loose near-chance: 40–60%”.
4. Beneath the mixed-model card, add the compact formula text:
   “guess ~ true source + item features + crossed expert/item effects”.

COMPOSITION RULES
- The lock/unlock mechanism must make the timing of unblinding visually explicit.
- Do not show actual expert or student accuracy values in this workflow panel.
- Do not color guesses as if they were true sources.
- Use meaningful icons only: locked key, shuffled cards, clinician, student group, binary choice, confusion matrix, forest plot.
- Fill the canvas efficiently and keep every arrow unambiguous and non-crossing.
```
